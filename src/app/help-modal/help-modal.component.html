Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

  1. What can I use this app for?

    You can generate QR codes from any text or link and send them by email as an image and PDF attachment.

  2. How do I save email addresses for future use?

    Use the "EMAIL AD" option in the header to add, view, or delete saved email addresses. These are stored locally on your device. In the "Select Email app" section, you will find more information on how to manage your email addresses.

  3. Why is the QR code cleared after I change the text?

    To ensure the QR code always matches the input, it is cleared and a notification is shown for 3 seconds if you modify the text after generating a QR code.

  4. How do I select or reset my default email app?

    When sending a QR code, you can choose your preferred email app. If you selected the wrong app as default, follow the "Reset default app" instructions in this help.

  5. How can I print the QR code in a specific size?

    Open the attached PDF or image, and use your printer’s scaling options or insert the image into a document to set the desired size. In the "How to print QR Code?" section, you will find detailed instructions.

  6. The keyboard covers the input field or buttons - how do I fix this?

    On smaller screens or older Android devices, the keyboard might cover important buttons or input fields.

    Solution:

    1. Tap the three dots (...) right above the keyboard
    2. Select "Modes" (Modi)
    3. Choose "Floating keyboard" (Schwebende Tastatur)

    This makes the keyboard smaller and moveable, so you can access all app functions properly.

  7. Is my data private?

    Yes, all data is stored locally on your device. No data is sent to external servers.

  8. How do I change the app language?

    Tap the language option in the header to switch between English and German. The change takes effect immediately.

  9. Where can I find the app version and update history?

    Scroll to the "App Version & History" section or tap the "Release Notes" button for details.

  10. Why is the email body sometimes missing when I send a QR code?

    Some third-party email apps (such as Bluemail) may not display the pre-filled email body text when sending emails with attachments. This is a limitation of those email apps. We recommend using standard email apps like Gmail or Outlook for the best experience.

  11. Why are the buttons in the title bar sometimes not displayed correctly?

    In rare cases, the buttons in the title bar may not be displayed correctly. This usually happens if your phone does not have the latest operating system installed. Please perform a system update to ensure the buttons are displayed correctly again.

  12. Why do I get "Storage Permission Required" error and how do I fix it?

    If you see this error when saving or mailing QR codes, you need to grant storage permissions to the app:

    Solution:

    1. Go to Settings
    2. Select Apps
    3. Find z-control QR Code Generator
    4. Tap Permissions
    5. Enable Storage permission

    This error typically occurs on older Android versions (e.g., Android 9). When you first start the app, you'll be asked to allow permissions to access photos, media, and files. If you previously denied permissions and selected "Don't ask again", you'll get this error and need to manually enable them in your device settings.

What can the app do?

A QR code is generated from the text you enter and sent by Email. The text is unformatted and can be up to {{ maxInputLength }} characters long. You can enter plain text, a web link, or a combination of both.

The QR code is attached to the Email as an image and PDF, and the entered text is included in the Email. This way you can immediately see which text is contained in the QR code, in case you create multiple QR codes.

To avoid having to enter the Email address every time you send an Email, you can save Email addresses.

Details on generating QR code

  1. Enter single or multi-line text or link to a web page (e.g. https://de.wikipedia.org).

    A new line or blank lines can be inserted by pressing the Enter key one or more times. Only uppercase formatting is supported.

    Blank lines or spaces are only allowed at the beginning or within the text. If they are entered at the end, they will be removed from the input field during generation, and a notification is displayed for 3 seconds.

    The length of the entered text and the maximum allowed ({{ maxInputLength }}) are displayed below the input field.

    You can also copy and paste text, but only the first 1000 characters will be copied.

  2. Press the generate button.
    The generated QR code will then be displayed.

  3. Using the Email QR code button, the standard selection dialog of Android devices opens and you can select your Email app (e.g. Gmail or Outlook). The exact procedure is described in the section Select Email app.

    A new Email is then created with the entered text and the QR codes are attached to the Email as png and pdf files. The Email can be edited if necessary and sent with the arrow on the right in the top right corner.

    Sending can be repeated as often as desired by pressing Email QR code again and changing the recipient.

    Content of the Email

    Recipient: The recipient(s) are automatically pre-filled if they were saved using EMAIL AD. Otherwise, they must be entered.

    Subject: Generated z-control QR Code of your text with the length of the entered text. Example: Generated z-control QR Code of your text with 512 characters

    Text: The entered text contained in the QR codes.

  4. Change or delete text.
    The text can also be changed after the QR code has been generated. However, the QR code will then be cleared and a notification will be displayed for 3 seconds. You must then generate a new QR code from the changed text.

    To allow the text to be changed even after sending, e.g. for small adjustments, the text is not cleared after sending.

    The text can be cleared using the Clear input button. The generated QR code will then no longer be displayed.

Here you can switch between QR code creation and Email address management. A switch is only possible if no text has been entered yet.

All previously saved email addresses are displayed. New email addresses can be added and previously saved email addresses can be deleted.

The input field can be deleted using the Delete input button and the Email address management can be canceled at any time using the Back to QR Codebutton.

If a language other than German or English is set, the app will be displayed in English.

The language can be changed by pressing the language selection. The change takes effect immediately.

Select Email app

By pressing the Email QR code button, the standard selection dialog of Android devices opens and you can select your Email app (e.g. Gmail or Outlook). The options Just Once and Always are offered.

If you select Always, this selected program will always be used and the Email app will open immediately. If you select Just Once, you will be prompted to select an Email app each time you send an Email.

Important: You must select a valid Email app (e.g. Gmail or Outlook) to use all functions of the z-control QR Code Generator app. If you accidentally selected another program with the Always option, you can reset this selection in the Android settings. This is explained in the next section Reset default app.

Reset default app

If you accidentally selected a program that is not an Email app (e.g. WhatsApp) as the default for sending emails and chose the Always option, you can reset this selection in the Android settings.

Step-by-step instructions
  1. Open the settings on your Android device.
  2. Scroll down and tap on "Apps" or "Applications".
  3. Find and select the program from the list (e.g. WhatsApp) that you selected for sending emails.
  4. Scroll down to "Set as default" (it may also say "Open by default" or similar) and tap on it.
  5. Tap on "Clear defaults" or "Reset defaults".
  6. Close the settings on your Android device.

The link is now removed and the next time you open a mailto link or click on Send Email, you will be asked again which app should open the link.

How to print QR Code in a specific size?

There are several ways to print the QR code in a specific size. This depends on the installed printer software and printer. This example is intended to help you print the QR code in your desired size.

  • Printing the PDF document
    1. Open the PDF document on your device
    2. Select Print in your PDF viewer
    3. Calculate the scaling from the printout according to the email attachment and your desired QR code size
    4. In the print dialog, select the option Custom scale and enter the scaling factor
  • Printing the image (filename.png)
    1. Open the image on your device
    2. Copy the image
    3. Open a word processor (e.g. Google Docs or Word)
    4. Paste the image into the document
    5. Select the image and choose the option Size and Position from the context menu
    6. You can now either enter the height and width in cm or, as with the PDF document, calculate and enter the scaling

QR codes explained

What are QR codes?

QR codes (Quick Response Codes) are two-dimensional barcodes that store information in a square pattern. They can be read with a camera or QR code scanner and allow quick access to information, websites, or actions.

How can I use them privately?

In everyday life, QR codes offer many possible uses:

  • Contact information: QR codes can store your contact details, which others can scan to contact you.
  • Links to websites: You can create QR codes that link to websites to share information quickly.
  • Wi-Fi access: A QR code can be used to quickly and easily provide guests with access details for your home Wi-Fi.
  • Digital photo albums & recipes: A QR code on a greeting card can share a link to an online photo album or a digitized family recipe.
  • Pets & valuables: On pet tags, keychains, or bicycles, a QR code with contact details can help with recovery in case of loss.
  • Emergency and health data: For example, on a medical bracelet, so important information is quickly available in an emergency.
  • Social media & online profiles: Share personal profiles, playlists, or curated content with friends or family.

QR codes are practical, flexible tools for easily sharing and using digital content, contact details, or personal information in everyday life.

How can companies use QR codes?

QR codes are used by companies in many ways to make digital content easily accessible and to strengthen customer loyalty. Typical applications include:

  • Marketing and advertising: QR codes on product packaging, flyers, posters, or business cards lead customers directly to websites, offers, social media profiles, or app downloads, increasing reach and interaction.
  • Customer service and information: QR codes allow quick access to user manuals, product videos, or further details, improving user convenience.
  • Events and admission control: QR codes serve as digital tickets and enable efficient check-in at events.
  • Payments and vouchers: They facilitate contactless payments and redeeming discount coupons.
  • Logistics and product tracking: QR codes support tracking goods in supply chains and promote transparency in the process.
  • Digital business cards: Contact details can be quickly and accurately transferred to smartphones via QR code.
  • Customer feedback and interaction: Companies use QR codes to make reviews, surveys, or calendar entries directly available.

QR codes enable a seamless transition from the offline to the online world, always providing clear added value to the user. It is important to make the benefit of the code visible and to optimize the target pages for mobile devices.

QR Code kurz erklärt

Was sind QR Codes?

QR Codes (Quick Response Codes) sind zweidimensionale Strichcodes, die Informationen in einem quadratischen Muster speichern. Sie können mit einer Kamera oder einem QR Code-Scanner gelesen werden und ermöglichen den schnellen Zugriff auf Informationen, Websites oder Aktionen.

Wie kann ich sie privat nutzen?

Im privaten Bereich bieten QR Codes zahlreiche Anwendungsmöglichkeiten:

  • Kontaktinformationen: QR Codes können Ihre Kontaktdaten speichern, die andere Personen scannen können, um Sie zu kontaktieren.
  • Links zu Websites: Sie können QR Codes erstellen, die auf Websites verweisen, um Informationen schnell zu teilen.
  • WLAN-Zugang: Ein QR Code kann genutzt werden, um Gästen schnell und einfach Zugangsdaten zum heimischen WLAN bereitzustellen.
  • Digitale Fotoalben & Rezepte: Über einen QR Code auf einer Grußkarte kann ein Link zu einem Online-Fotoalbum oder einem digitalisierten Familienrezept geteilt werden.
  • Haustiere & Wertgegenstände: Auf Hundemarken, Schlüsselanhängern oder Fahrrädern kann ein QR Code mit Kontaktdaten angebracht werden, um im Verlustfall die Rückgabe zu erleichtern.
  • Notfall- und Gesundheitsdaten: Einsatz z.B. auf einem medizinischen Armband, sodass im Notfall wichtige Informationen schnell zur Verfügung stehen.
  • Social Media & Online-Auftritte: Teilen persönlicher Profile, Playlists oder kuratierter Inhalte mit Freunden oder Familie

QR Codes sind praktische, flexible Werkzeuge, um im Alltag unkompliziert digitale Inhalte, Kontaktdaten oder persönliche Informationen zu teilen und zu nutzen.

Wie können Firmen QR Codes nutzen?

QR-Codes werden von Unternehmen vielseitig eingesetzt, um digitale Inhalte einfach zugänglich zu machen und die Kundenbindung zu stärken. Typische Anwendungen sind:

  • Marketing und Werbung: QR-Codes auf Produktverpackungen, Flyern, Plakaten oder Visitenkarten führen Kunden direkt zu Websites, Angeboten, Social-Media-Profilen oder App-Downloads und erhöhen so die Reichweite und Interaktion.
  • Kundenservice und Information: Durch QR-Codes können Bedienungsanleitungen, Produktvideos oder weitere Details schnell abgerufen werden, was den Nutzerkomfort steigert.
  • Veranstaltungen und Eintrittskontrolle: QR-Codes dienen als digitale Tickets und ermöglichen effizientes Einchecken bei Events.
  • Zahlungsverkehr und Gutscheine: Sie erleichtern kontaktloses Bezahlen und das Einlösen von Rabattcoupons.
  • Logistik und Produktverfolgung: QR-Codes unterstützen die Warenverfolgung in Lieferketten und fördern die Transparenz im Prozess.
  • Digitale Visitenkarten: Kontaktdaten lassen sich per QR-Code schnell und fehlerfrei auf Smartphones übertragen.
  • Kundenfeedback und Interaktion: Unternehmen nutzen QR-Codes, um Bewertungen, Umfragen oder Kalendereinträge direkt verfügbar zu machen.

Durch QR-Codes gelingt der fließende Übergang von Offline- zu Online-Welt, wobei der Nutzer stets einen klaren Mehrwert erhält. Wichtig ist, den Nutzen des Codes sichtbar zu machen und die Zielseiten für mobile Geräte zu optimieren.

Technische Informationen

Die App ist Open Source und der Quellcode ist auf GitHub verfügbar, sodass Sie die Funktionsweise der App und den Umgang mit Ihren Daten jederzeit überprüfen können.

Spracheingabe: Wenn Sie in den Handy-Einstellungen die Spracheingabe aktivieren, erscheint im Tastaturfeld ein Mikrofon-Symbol. Durch Tippen auf dieses Symbol wechseln Sie zur Spracheingabe. Sie können flexibel zwischen Tastatur und Spracheingabe wechseln. Die Bedienung entspricht der üblichen Spracheingabe-Funktion Ihres Handys.

Scannen des generierten QR Codes: Der erfasste Text kann bis zu {{ maxInputLength }} Zeichen lang sein. Beachten Sie: Je mehr Text der QR Code enthält, desto länger dauert der Scan-Vorgang. Für sehr lange Texte empfehle ich eine spezielle Scanner-App. QR Codes mit bis zu 500 Zeichen können in der Regel problemlos mit der Kamera-App Ihres Handys gescannt werden. Bei den meisten modernen Smartphones ist die QR Code-Scan-Funktion bereits in der Standard-Kamera-App integriert.

Erfasste Mailadressen werden im Handy in Local Storage (IndexedDB bzw. Local Storage) gespeichert.

Die generierten QR Code Dateien werden dem E-Mail als Anhang übergeben und im Handy im Documents Ordner temporär gespeichert. Nach 3 Stunden oder wenn Sie die App neu starten werden sie automatisch gelöscht.

Die QR Code Datei Namen enthalten den Text qrcode und den Zeitstempel der Generierung und haben folgendes Format:
qrcode_YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS.png
qrcode_YYYYMMDD_HHMMSS.pdf
